## Why this matters

A website can look “fine” while quietly losing enquiries. Buyers compare you on mobile, skim headings in seconds and leave if the offer is unclear. These signs help you decide whether a refresh is cosmetic or commercially necessary.

## 1. Mobile visitors bounce quickly

If analytics show high mobile exit rates on key pages, the layout, tap targets or load speed may be failing real users. Google also uses mobile experience as a ranking signal.

**What to do:** Test core journeys on a phone: homepage, top service page and contact. If anything feels cramped or slow, treat it as a redesign trigger.

## 2. Your services are hard to explain in one scroll

Buyers should understand what you do, who it is for and why you are credible without hunting through menus. Vague homepages and thin service pages are common on older sites.

Pair a redesign with clearer [web design](/services/web-design) structure: dedicated service routes, proof blocks and internal links to [case studies](/case-studies).

## 3. Page speed scores stay poor

Slow sites hurt both SEO and trust. Heavy page builders, unoptimised images and too many scripts are frequent causes.

Read our [website performance guide](/blog/website-performance-optimisation-guide-2026) or consider a [Next.js development](/services/nextjs-development) build if the current platform cannot be tuned.

## 4. You are embarrassed to share the URL

If staff avoid sending the link or you apologise before meetings, the brand layer is misaligned with how good the business actually is.

A [website redesign](/services/website-redesign) should close that gap, not with decoration alone, but with messaging, hierarchy and proof.

## 5. Enquiries mention confusion

Sales notes like “I wasn’t sure if you cover X” or “I couldn’t find pricing guidance” point to information architecture problems. Fix the sitemap before polishing visuals.

Use our [conversion-focused homepage checklist](/blog/conversion-focused-homepage-checklist-uk-businesses) as a benchmark.

## 6. Competitors look sharper in search results

Compare your listing snippet and landing experience with two local rivals. If their titles, descriptions and pages answer buyer questions faster, you are at a disadvantage.

[Local SEO](/services/local-seo) and strong area pages, such as [web design in Sheffield](/areas-we-cover/sheffield), help when you compete regionally.

## 7. The site cannot grow without breaking

Adding a case study, new service or blog post should not require a developer every time, but it also should not mean bolting on plugins until performance collapses.

Plan for a platform that balances editor flexibility with speed. Our comparison of [WordPress vs Next.js for business websites](/blog/wordpress-vs-nextjs-for-business-websites) explains the trade-offs.

## Redesign vs refresh

| Situation | Sensible approach |
|-----------|-------------------|
| Brand and offer unchanged, layout dated | Targeted page updates |
| New services, locations or audience | Structural redesign + SEO migration |
| Poor Core Web Vitals, security issues | Rebuild on a modern stack |
| Rebrand or merger | Full redesign with content rewrite |

## FAQs

### How long does a UK business website redesign take?

Most focused projects run roughly **8–14 weeks** from discovery to launch, depending on page count, content readiness and integrations. See our [project timeline guide](/blog/website-project-timeline-what-uk-businesses-should-expect).

### Will we lose Google rankings?

Not if redirects, metadata and internal links are handled properly. Treat SEO migration as part of the brief, not an afterthought.
