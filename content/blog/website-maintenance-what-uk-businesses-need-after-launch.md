## Launch is the starting line

A website that wins enquiries needs ongoing care. Neglecting updates, security and content gives competitors room to catch up, especially in search.

## Core maintenance tasks

### Security and platform updates

- Apply framework, plugin or hosting patches promptly  
- Monitor SSL certificates and domain renewals  
- Review form spam protection and backup schedules  

### Uptime and performance

- Monthly checks on Core Web Vitals  
- Image and script audits after marketing adds tracking pixels  
- See [website performance optimisation](/blog/website-performance-optimisation-guide-2026)

### Content freshness

- New [case studies](/case-studies) when projects complete  
- Service page updates when offers change  
- Blog or [guides](/guides) posts that support SEO clusters  

### SEO monitoring

- Search Console for coverage and query shifts  
- Broken link and redirect checks after URL changes  
- [Local SEO](/services/local-seo) listings kept consistent (NAP, categories, photos)

## Retainer vs ad-hoc support

| Model | Best when |
|-------|-----------|
| **Monthly retainer** | You publish regularly, run campaigns, need priority fixes |
| **Ad-hoc hours** | Stable site, few changes, internal team handles copy |
| **Quarterly review** | Mature site, focus on analytics and SEO opportunities |

## Typical UK budget bands (indicative)

- **Essential care**: security, backups, small fixes at a modest monthly fee  
- **Growth retainer**: content, SEO tweaks, CRO tests at a higher rate, tied to goals  
- **Rebuild triggers**: platform end-of-life, major rebrand, persistent speed issues  

Align spend with [what you paid to build](/blog/how-much-does-a-business-website-cost-in-the-uk). Maintenance is usually a fraction of project cost but protects that investment.

## What in-house teams can own

- Blog drafts and social promotion  
- Uploading team photos and news  
- Answering form enquiries quickly (conversion matters as much as traffic)

Agency or studio support works best for structural changes, technical SEO and [Next.js](/services/nextjs-development) updates.

## FAQs

### Is maintenance included in the build?

Clarify before signing. Devora projects include launch support; ongoing care is agreed separately.

### How often should we publish content?

Consistency beats volume. One useful article per month plus annual service reviews is a solid baseline for many SMEs.

### When is a redesign cheaper than maintaining?

If the stack is unsafe, slow or impossible to edit, compare [redesign signals](/blog/signs-your-business-website-needs-a-redesign-uk) against another year of patches.
